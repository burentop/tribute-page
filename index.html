C.S. Lewis

An Irish Gentleman

A brief look at Lewis' life:

  • 1898 - Born on November 29 in Belfast, Ireland
  • 1905 - Family moves into new house on the outskirts of Belfast, "Little Lea"
  • 1908 - Lewis' mother dies of cancer
  • 1910 - Lewis enrolls at Campbell College, Belfast as a boarding student
  • 1911 - One year later, due to respiratory difficulties, Lewis is sent to a 'health resort' in Malvern, England
  • 1917 - Enrolls in University College, Oxford; and is subsequently drafted into the armed forces for World War I
  • 1918 - Lewis is injured during the Battle of Arras.
  • 1919 - Lewis is discharged from service and resumes studies at Oxford.
  • 1925 - Lewis is elected as a Fellow of Magdalen College, Oxford - where he remains for 29 years.
  • 1931 - Largely due to his friendship with J.R.R. Tolkien, Lewis becomes a Christian.
  • 1937 - Lewis received the Gollancz Prize for Literature
  • 1941 - Lewis' 'Screwtape Letters' is published in the Guardian
  • 1946 - Lewis is awarded an honorary Doctor of Divinity by the University of St. Andrews
  • 1950 - The first book of the Chronicles of Narnia series is published
  • 1952 - Lewis meets Joy Gresham for the first time
  • 1955 - Lewis leaves Oxford to accept the Chair of Medeival Literature at Cambridge
  • 1956 - Lewis marries Joy Gresham
  • 1960 - Lewis' wife, Joy, dies of cancer at the age of 45
  • 1963 - Lewis dies on November 22
